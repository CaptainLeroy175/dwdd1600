Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence. He wrote the original draft of the document, which laid out the philosophical justifications for the colonies' decision to separate from Great Britain.
John Adams
Advocate for independence and a key figure in the Continental Congress. Adams played a crucial role in pushing for the declaration of independence and later served as the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Diplomat and elder statesman. Franklin was instrumental in securing foreign support, particularly from France, for the American Revolution. He was also a key member of the Continental Congress.
Samuel Adams
Radical leader and organizer of resistance to British rule. He helped organize the Boston Tea Party and was a leading advocate for independence, helping to rally public sentiment in favor of the Revolution.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress. His bold and large signature on the Declaration became iconic, symbolizing defiance against British rule. Hancock is often remembered for his role in financing and supporting the revolution.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the motion for independence in Congress. Before the Declaration was signed, Lee's resolution in the Continental Congress called for the colonies to declare independence, setting the stage for the document's drafting.
James Madison
Future fourth President of the U.S. and “Father of the Constitution.” Though Madison wasn’t a direct signer (he was a bit younger), he was deeply involved in the political processes leading up to independence and was a key figure in drafting the Constitution.
Thomas Paine
Philosopher and writer. Paine’s pamphlet Common Sense was a powerful argument for independence that influenced public opinion, even though he wasn’t part of the Continental Congress.

These individuals, along with the rest of the 56 signers, were key figures in the founding of the United States. Their decision to sign the Declaration was a bold act of defiance that set the stage for the American Revolution.
